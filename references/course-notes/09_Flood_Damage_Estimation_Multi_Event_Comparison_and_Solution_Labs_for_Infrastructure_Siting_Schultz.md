## Titel/Title

Flood Damage Estimation, Multi-Event Comparison, and Solution Labs for Infrastructure Siting

## Zweck/Purpose

This project aims to map the impacts of one or more recent flood events and translate the results into a compact solution-lab exercise for resilient planning. The study should move beyond flood extent alone and ask which locations are repeatedly exposed, which assets are affected, and where suitable sites exist for infrastructure or adaptation measures.

The topic is linked to existing FRAME and flood-risk WebGIS material and is suitable for students who want to combine remote sensing, exposure analysis, and spatial decision support in one applied workflow.

## Vorgeschlagene Vorgehensweise/Proposed approach

Delineate flood extent or flood-related damage proxies for one event or two comparable events using Sentinel-1 and, where feasible, supporting optical information. Overlay the hazard footprint with buildings, transport lines, land use, or other exposed assets to estimate relative impact and identify recurrent hotspots. Develop a compact multi-criteria suitability analysis for resilient siting based on hazard avoidance, elevation, accessibility, land cover, and practical constraints. Present the results as a map package or as a lightweight WebGIS concept.

## Erwartete Ergebnisse/Expected outcome

- An event-specific or comparative flood impact assessment based on open EO data.
- An exposure-based estimate of relative flood damage or affected assets.
- A suitability map for resilient infrastructure siting or adaptation measures.
- A transparent multi-criteria workflow with explicit assumptions and limitations.

## Software &amp; Data

Google Earth Engine, QGIS, and Python or R; Sentinel-1; optional Sentinel-2; DEM products such as FABDEM; OpenStreetMap; open land-cover and asset layers.
